Projects

Project 1: Train Refrigeration and Water Systems Control


Design and development of integrated refrigeration and water management systems for passenger trains. This system ensures seamless real-time communication with the train’s central control system for diagnostic monitoring and performance tracking, guaranteeing optimal operation of all onboard equipment and furniture. The control and monitoring interface is provided via:


  • **HMI** (Human-Machine Interface): A user-friendly display allowing operators to manage, visualize, and adjust system parameters.

  • **SCADA** (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition): Provides centralized real-time monitoring, data acquisition, and remote control of multiple subsystems.

    • The project involved comprehensive factory acceptance tests (FAT), rigorous commissioning, and continuous operational follow-up to ensure system reliability, safety, and compliance with railway standards.

Project 2: Toilet Water, Door, and Device Control Systems

Development of automated control systems for onboard toilet facilities, including water distribution, door mechanisms, and auxiliary devices. The system communicates continuously with the train’s central monitoring system, enabling real-time diagnostics and automatic response to operational issues. Key features include:


  • Automatic system shutdown in case of freezing or abnormal conditions.

  • Configurable operation via PLC-integrated LED interfaces for local visualization and adjustments.

    • The implementation included factory testing, on-site commissioning, and ongoing performance monitoring, ensuring high reliability, safety, and seamless integration with the train’s overall control network.

Project 3: Pharmaceutical and Medical Device Production Machinery Control

Design and implementation of advanced control systems for pharmaceutical and medical device manufacturing machinery, focusing on operator safety, process automation, and regulatory compliance with GMP (Good Manufacturing Practices). The system manages multiple production lines, providing real-time monitoring, automated controls, and process optimization to maintain product quality and safety standards. The control and monitoring interface is provided via:


  • **HMI** (Human-Machine Interface): Allows operators to visualize and interact with production processes, adjust parameters, and respond to alerts in real-time.

  • **MES** (Manufacturing Execution System) Integration: Ensures production data is collected, analyzed, and communicated to enterprise-level systems for traceability, reporting, and operational optimization.

    • The project phases included factory acceptance tests (FAT), detailed commissioning, and continuous operational monitoring, ensuring robust performance, compliance with industry standards, and operator safety throughout the production process.
